## Notes

> Sections 1-3 on the midterm; lectures 1-9 and problems 1-4.

### 1. Suffix Notation

1. [[Suffix Notation]] (index notation) - the basic notation used throughout Tensor Analysis.
2. [[Kronecker Delta]] (substitution tensor) - how we write dot products, among other relationships.
3. [[Alternating Tensor]] / [[Levi-Civita Symbol]] - how we write cross products, among other relationships.

### 2. Vector Differential Operators

1. [[Gradient]].
2. [[Divergence]].
3. [[Curl]].

### 3. Local Coordinate Transform

1. [[Coordinate Transformation Matrix]] - the rotation matrix $L_{ij}$ bridging two orthonormal bases.
2. [[Jacobian]] - partial derivatives of one coordinate system with respect to another, $\frac{\partial x_{i}'}{\partial x_{j}}=L_{ij}$.
3. [[Basis Transformation]] - formal definitions of scalars and vectors via their transformation rules.

### 4. Tensors

1. [[rank]] - the number of free indices of a tensor.
2. [[Tensor Transformation Rule]] - one factor of $L$ per free index.
3. [[Metric Tensor]] - $g_{ik}$ determines arc length, angles, and raising/lowering.
4. [[Covariant and Contravariant Components]] - two ways to expand a vector in a generalised basis.
5. [[Quotient Rule]] - shortcut for proving tensor character via arbitrary contractions.
6. [[Associated Tensors]] - tensors related by raising/lowering indices.
7. [[Symmetry and Antisymmetry]] - frame-independent decomposition of rank-2 tensors.

### 5. Tensors in a Generalised Coordinate System

1. [[Mixed Components]] - the four flavours $A_{ik}, A^{ik}, A^{\cdot k}_{i}, A^{i}_{\cdot k}$ of a rank-2 tensor.
2. [[Index Raising and Lowering]] - the metric-tensor recipe for converting between component types.

### 6. Tensor Algebra

1. [[Tensor Addition]] - componentwise, requires matching rank and structure.
2. [[Outer Product]] - $A\otimes B$, rank adds, structures concatenate.
3. [[Contraction]] - summing two indices, drops rank by 2; pair upper with lower in curvilinear coordinates.

### 7. Tensor Fields

1. [[Local Basis]] - basis vectors that vary with position.
2. [[Covariant Differentiation]] - partial derivative + one $\Gamma$ per index.
3. [[Christoffel Symbols]] - the basis-change correction coefficients, determined by the metric.
4. [[Ricci's Theorem]] - the metric is covariantly constant, $g_{ik,\ell}=0$.
5. [[Riemann-Christoffel Tensor]] - detects curvature; $R^{r}_{ijk}=0 \iff$ Euclidean.
6. [[Ricci Tensor]] - contraction $R_{ij}=R^{r}_{irj}$; simpler curvature summary (used in general relativity).
