## Abstract

Formal methods, in general, are robust structures to filter out errors from widespread usage. For centuries, mathematics has relied on peer review as its main correctness filter, but peer review cannot catch all mistakes and is still subject to the fallible human nature that mathematics often seeks to avoid. In contrast, the newer field of software engineering has accumulated many more layers of mechanical checks: type systems, tests, continuous integration, fuzzing, among others. This report asks what equivalent robustness now exists in mathematics, how these methods demonstrate value, and where they add overhead without a matching gain in rigour.
