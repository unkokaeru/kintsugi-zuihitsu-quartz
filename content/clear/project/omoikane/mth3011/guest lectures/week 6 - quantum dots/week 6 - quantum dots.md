# Week 6 - Quantum Dots

Imagine that could have an invisible tattoo that stores information readable by your standard smartphone - this might sound like science fiction, but it's just one of the many applications of something that researchers at MIT have developed: quantum dots.

How is it possible, though? What even is a quantum dot? The first is a bit complicated, so let's start with the latter. Quantum dots are something called "semi-conductor crystals", measuring roughly one-thousandth the width of a human hair - absolutely tiny! In fact, so small that the physics we learned in high school no longer works, and we have to use something called "quantum mechanics" instead, hence the name. This makes them harder to work with, but gives them properties that are otherwise impossible with bigger materials.
