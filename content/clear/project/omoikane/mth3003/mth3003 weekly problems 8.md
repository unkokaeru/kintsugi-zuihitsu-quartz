## 8.3. Action of $S_{6}$ on $2$-Subsets

> [!question]
> Let $G = S_{6}$ and let
>
> $$
> X = \{\{1, 2\}, \{1, 3\}, \{1, 4\}, \{1, 5\}, \{1, 6\}, \{2, 3\}, \{2, 4\}, \{2, 5\}, \{2, 6\}, \{3, 4\}, \{3, 5\}, \{3, 6\}, \{4, 5\}, \{4, 6\}, \{5, 6\}\}.
> $$
>
> Suppose $\lambda(g)\{i, j\} = \{g i, g j\}$ for all $\{i, j\} \in X$ and all $g \in G$.
>
> Prove that $\lambda$ is an action of $G$ on $X$, and hence that $X$ is a $G$-set.

## 8.8. Homomorphisms into $\mathrm{Sym}(X)$ as Actions

> [!question]
> Let $\lambda \colon G \to \mathrm{Sym}(X)$ be a homomorphism.
>
> Prove that $\lambda$ is an action of $G$ on $X$.
>
> *Note: In lectures we proved that every action is a homomorphism into $\mathrm{Sym}(X)$. Here you are proving that every homomorphism into $\mathrm{Sym}(X)$ is an action, so actions on $X$ and homomorphisms into $\mathrm{Sym}(X)$ are essentially the same thing.*
