# Symmetric group

For a set $X$, the **symmetric group** $\mathrm{Sym}(X)$ is the group of all [[Bijection]] $X\to X$ under composition.

When $X=\{1,2,\ldots,n\}$, write $S_{n}=\mathrm{Sym}(\{1,\ldots,n\})$ - the **symmetric group of degree $n$**. Its elements are the [[Permutation]] of $\{1,\ldots,n\}$.

## Order

$$
|S_{n}|=n!.
$$

## Generators

$S_{n}$ is generated by:

- All transpositions $\{(i\,j)\}$ - a *transposition* is a 2-cycle.
- The two elements $(1\,2)$ and $(1\,2\,\cdots\,n)$ (a transposition and a long cycle).
- Adjacent transpositions $\{(i\,i+1):1\leq i\leq n-1\}$.

## [[Cayley's theorem]]

Every group $G$ embeds into $\mathrm{Sym}(G)$ via the regular action. So **every finite group of order $n$ is a subgroup of $S_{n}$** (up to isomorphism).

## Structure

- **Centre:** $Z(S_{n})=\{e\}$ for $n\geq 3$. Trivial centre.
- **Normal subgroups:** for $n\geq 5$, the only normal subgroups of $S_{n}$ are $\{e\}, A_{n}, S_{n}$ - i.e. $S_{n}/A_{n}\cong C_{2}$ is the only non-trivial quotient.
- **Conjugacy classes:** indexed by **cycle shapes** (= integer partitions of $n$). See [[Conjugacy]].
- **[[Signature]]** $\sigma\colon S_{n}\to\{\pm 1\}$ is the unique non-trivial homomorphism to a smaller group; its kernel is $A_{n}$, the [[Alternating group]].

## Small Cases

- $S_{1}=\{e\}$, trivial.
- $S_{2}\cong C_{2}$, two elements.
- $S_{3}$, six elements: identity, three transpositions, two 3-cycles. Smallest non-abelian group.
- $S_{4}$, twenty-four elements; isomorphic to the rotation group of the cube.
- $S_{5}$, $120$ elements. Not solvable (Galois-theoretic obstruction to quintic formula).

## Subgroups

Subgroups of $S_{n}$ are called **permutation groups**. By Cayley, every group is a permutation group.

Some standard examples:

- $A_{n}$ (alternating group), index 2.
- Cyclic subgroups $\langle\pi\rangle$ generated by individual permutations.
- Dihedral group $D_{2n}\leq S_{n}$, the symmetries of an $n$-gon.
- Wreath products and direct products of smaller symmetric groups.
