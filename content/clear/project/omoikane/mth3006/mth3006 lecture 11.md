## How Do You Derive the Heat Equation?

Assuming the simplest case for a one-dimensional rod of length $L$:

1. A rod made of a single homogeneous conducting material, e.g. metal.
2. Heat flows only in the $x$-direction, e.g. the rod is laterally insulated.
3. Temperature is constant at all points of a cross-section, e.g. the rod is thin.
