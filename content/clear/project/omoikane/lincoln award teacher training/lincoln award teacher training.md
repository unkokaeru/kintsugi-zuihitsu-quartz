### Reflection 1 – Welcome Session

1. **What are you most looking forward to about the Lincoln Award for Teaching Programme?**
   I'm most looking forward to observing lessons from a teacher's perspective, rather than that of a student; not just the pedagogical techniques employed, but the nuances of how the teacher behaves to control the class, too. I think that'll really help to cement the theory that I know into real practices that I might even be able to use towards the end of the week – another exciting thing!
2. **Is there anything that you perhaps feel nervous about?**
   I have a lot of experience in one-to-one teaching, but I'm a bit nervous about teaching a larger group – I'm grateful for the support towards this, though, and it's also something I'm excited to overcome! I think the first day is also something a bit nerve-racking, just being a new environment in a manner I'm unfamiliar with, but I'm sure that'll pass quickly.
3. **What key learning/experiences are you hoping to cover during your time at the school?**
   I really hope I get to understand the methods employed in real teaching environments, to teach and control a classroom, as well as to be able to reflect on how I've been taught in the past and how it compares.
4. **Is there anything you would like use to cover on the final day of the programme that would help you moving forward?**
   Honestly, I can't think of anything now. The programme looks well thought through and put together, and I look forward to the satisfaction of completing it! Even the group presentation at the end seems interesting to be able to see how my peers found the experience and what they learned, too, and how that compares with my experience.
5. **Please make a note of any other reflective points that you would like to note at this stage.**
   No other reflective points at this stage.
