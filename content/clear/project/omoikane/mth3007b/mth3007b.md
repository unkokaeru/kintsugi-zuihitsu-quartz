### 2. Numerical Solutions of Partial Differential Equations

1. [[Heat equation]] - the canonical parabolic PDE; basis of the diffusion / Laplace / Poisson problems.
2. [[Boundary conditions]] - Dirichlet, Neumann, mixed; imaginary-point trick for Neumann.
3. Finite-difference schemes:
	1. [[FTCS scheme]] - explicit, conditionally stable ($r\leq 1/2$).
	2. [[BTCS scheme]] - implicit, unconditionally stable, tridiagonal solve per step.
	3. [[Crank-Nicolson scheme]] - second-order in both space and time, unconditionally stable.
4. Stability analysis:
	1. [[von Neumann stability]] - Fourier-mode amplification analysis.
	2. [[CFL condition]] - hyperbolic-PDE stability bound.
5. Steady-state / elliptic:
	1. [[Laplace equation]] - $\nabla^{2}u=0$; pure BVP, steady state of diffusion.
	2. [[Laplacian difference equation]] - 5-point stencil on a square grid.
6. Iterative linear solvers:
	1. [[Liebmann's method]] - Gauss-Seidel applied to Laplace/Poisson.
	2. [[Diagonal dominance]] - sufficient condition for Jacobi/Gauss-Seidel convergence.

### 3. Elements of Stochastic Methods

1. [[Pseudo-random number generation]] - seeds, distributions, statistical quality.
2. [[Monte Carlo integration]] - $O(1/\sqrt{N})$ rate, dimension-independent; wins above $D=2$.
3. [[Random walks]] - discrete predecessor of the Wiener process.
4. [[Wiener process]] - continuous-time Brownian motion; equivalent to diffusion equation with delta initial condition.
5. [[Stochastic differential equation]] - drift + diffusion in differential form.
6. [[Euler-Maruyama scheme]] - first-order discretisation of SDEs; the stochastic analogue of Euler.
7. [[Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process]] - Wiener with linear restoring force; bounded equilibrium variance.
8. [[First-passage time]] - threshold-crossing times; heavy-tailed in the tail-crossing regime.
