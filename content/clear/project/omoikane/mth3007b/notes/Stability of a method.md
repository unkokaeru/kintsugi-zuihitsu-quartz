# Stability of a Method

A method $T$ is stable if, for a stable ODE, two slightly different initial conditions $y_A(0)$ and $y_B(0) = y_A(0) + \epsilon$ produce solutions $y_{T,A}(t)$ and $y_{T,B}(t)$ whose difference stays bounded - i.e., there exists $M(\epsilon)$ such that $|y_{T,A}(t) - y_{T,B}(t)| < M$ for all $t > 0$.
